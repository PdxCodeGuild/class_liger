Caesar Marcus Aurelius Antoninus Augustus

The last of the 'Five Good Emperors' of Rome

26 April 121 - 17 March 180

Marcus Aurelius ruled Rome as emperor from 161 until his death in 180 AD. Under his rule, according to noted historian Edward Gibbon, "the Roman Empire was ruled by absolute power, under the guidance of wisdom and virtue". He is one of only two emperors for whom we have existing writings (the other being Julius Ceasar). His Meditations is a treatise on Stoic philosophy that is still widely read, and some of his personal letters still exist that provide insight into his noble character.

Upon the death of Antoninus Pius, Marcus assumed the role of co-emperor with Lucious Verus, becoming sole emperor after Lucious' death in 169. During his reign, the empire faced many threats including floods and famine, pestilence, a tribal rebellion in Germany, and a military uprising in Asia. His victory over the Germanic Marcomanni, Quadi, and Sarmatians is memorialized in the The Column of Marcus Aurelius in Piazza Colonna, Rome, Italy.
